About

Mary Wanesia is a Jeweler, an Arizona native, and a member of the Navajo tribe. She specializes in creating beautiful contemporary takes on the traditional adornments worn by Navjo in ceramonies and in daily life.

Jewlry in the Amercas has an ancient multicolored stones and shells into beads and pendants. Olivella shell beads, dating from 6000 BCE, were found in Nevada; bone, antler, and possibly marine shell beads from 7000 BCE were found in Russell Cave Alabama; copper jewelry was traded from Lake Superior beginning in 3000 BCE; and stone beads were carved in Poverty Point Louisiana in 1500 BCE. Learn more.

Mary's jewelry has received recognition, and been featured in the National Museum of the American Indian, The New York Times Style Magazine, and Sedona Monthly. The collection has been sold across the United States and Europe and been carried by the retail stores of Bergdorf Goodman.
